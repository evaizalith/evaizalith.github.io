Publications

Ahadian, Powlison, Yang, Li, Xu, Guan. (Paper accepted; publication pending) "OAAgent: Multimodal LLM Agent for Predicting Knee Osteoarthritis Progression" IEEE/ACM CHASE

2025

Abstract: Osteoarthritis (OA) is a leading cause of disability worldwide, necessitating early prediction of its progression to enable timely interventions and personalized treatment strategies. This study introduces OAAgent, a novel AI-driven framework designed to forecast knee osteoarthritis progression by leveraging clinical and imaging data from the FNIH Biomarkers Consortium. Unlike traditional predictive models, OAAgent integrates multimodal data—including demographic variables, clinical features, and X-ray imaging biomarkers—into a reasoning based framework. This framework combines Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning with task execution, enabling interpretable decision pathways that align with clinical workflows. Experimental results demonstrate that OAAgent achieves competitive performance, with a prediction accuracy of 82.19% and robust sensitivity and specificity metrics, outperforming traditional models such as logistic regression. Beyond accuracy, OAAgent offers significant advantages in adaptability, transparency, and real-time decision-making, bridging the gap between AI-driven predictions and clinical usability. By dynamically integrating multimodal data and providing actionable insights, OAAgent positions itself as a transformative tool for advancing personalized medicine and improving OA management. Future work aims to expand its application to diverse datasets and real-world healthcare settings.
